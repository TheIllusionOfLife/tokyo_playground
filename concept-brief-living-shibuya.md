# Concept Brief: Living Shibuya

**Working Title:** Living Shibuya Update
**Genre & Platform:** Roblox party game / open-world social exploration (PC, Mobile, Console)
**Status:** Concept — ready for design breakdown

---

## High-Concept Pitch

Shibuya isn't just a game arena anymore — it's a living neighborhood. The city breathes with a day/night cycle, NPCs with routines, hidden stamps to discover, and spontaneous street festivals that erupt without warning. Riding Hachi through the city between matches becomes the coziest part of the game.

---

## The Three Pillars

### Pillar 1: Living City (Ambient Life)

Make the world feel inhabited and warm, so players want to stay between matches.

**Core Features:**

- **Day/Night Cycle** — Cosmetic lighting shifts over a 20-minute real-time cycle. Daytime: bright, bustling, shop signs lit. Nighttime: warm lantern glow, quieter streets, neon reflections on wet pavement. Affects NPC behavior and available interactions.

- **NPC Routines (8-12 NPCs at launch):**
  - Ramen vendor (daytime): waves at passing Hachi, occasionally tosses a free treat if you stop nearby
  - Street musician (evening): plays near Hachiko statue, Hachi sways to the music (idle anim)
  - Cat colony (rooftop, always): 3-5 cats that react to Hachi — hiss at Level 0, warm up at Level 2+, nap on Hachi at Level 4
  - Shopkeeper (daytime): sweeps sidewalk, comments on your cosmetics
  - Night owl photographer (nighttime): takes "photos" of players, gives small reward for posing
  - Delivery cyclist (random): zooms through streets, Hachi barks/reacts

- **Hachi Ambient Reactions:**
  - Near food stalls → nose wiggle + sniff animation
  - Near cats → ears perk up, tail wag
  - Near music → gentle body sway
  - Nighttime + idle → yawn → curl-up sleep pose
  - Rain (random weather event) → shakes off water, ears droop

- **Ambient Audio Layer:** Layered city sounds that shift with time of day — distant train announcements, crosswalk chimes, cicadas at dusk (夕方のヒグラシ), izakaya chatter at night.

### Pillar 2: Secret Shibuya (Discovery & Stamps)

Give exploration purpose through a stamp rally system and hidden vignettes.

**Core Features:**

- **Stamp Rally (スタンプラリー):**
  - 30+ hidden spots across Shibuya, each with a unique visual moment
  - Spots are small, hand-crafted vignettes: a rooftop garden with wind chimes, a tiny shrine behind a vending machine alley, a graffiti tunnel, a record shop interior you can peek into, a cat café window
  - Discovering a spot plays a gentle chime + stamps your rally card
  - Stamp card visible in UI — organized by district/area
  - Completing a stamp set (e.g., "Back Alley Set" — 5 stamps) unlocks an exclusive cosmetic

- **Exploration Gating via Hachi Abilities:**
  - Some spots require wall-run (Hachi Level 3+) to reach
  - Some require slide through narrow gaps
  - Some require double-jump to rooftop access
  - This gives Hachi evolution meaning OUTSIDE competitive matches
  - Players grind Hachi Ride not just to win, but to unlock exploration

- **Seasonal Stamps (4 per season):**
  - Cherry blossom viewing spot (spring)
  - Fireworks rooftop (summer)
  - Autumn leaf tunnel (fall)
  - Illumination walkway (winter)
  - Creates FOMO-free return incentive (stamps return each year)

- **Photo Mode:**
  - At any discovered stamp spot, players can enter photo mode
  - Pose Hachi (3-4 preset poses: sit, stand proud, sleep, silly)
  - Frame includes stamp name + date
  - Save to Roblox screenshot / share

### Pillar 3: Street Festival (Dynamic Micro-Events)

Break the "queue → match → wait" loop with spontaneous world events.

**Core Features:**

- **Event Cadence:** One micro-event every 12-18 minutes (randomized). Each lasts 2-4 minutes. Non-competitive — everyone participates together.

- **Event Types (6 at launch):**

  1. **Bon Odori Circle (盆踊り)**
     - A circle of NPC dancers appears in the scramble crossing
     - Players ride Hachi into the circle; simple rhythm prompt (press button on beat)
     - Reward: 5-15 Play Points + "Festival Glow" temporary trail (5 min)

  2. **Food Truck Arrival**
     - A special food truck parks at a random location
     - Audio cue: distant truck jingle (players learn to recognize it)
     - First 8 players to find it get a special Hachi treat (cosmetic food item that sits on Hachi's head for 10 min)
     - Everyone who visits gets a small point bonus

  3. **Fireworks Show (花火)**
     - Announced by a distant boom
     - Rooftop viewing spots glow — players who reach one see a 90-second fireworks display
     - Pure vibes. Small attendance reward (5 pts). Hachi looks up and wags tail.

  4. **Street Art Live**
     - An NPC artist appears at a wall and "paints" a mural in real-time (2 minutes)
     - Players who watch from start to finish get a commemorative stamp
     - Mural stays on the wall until next server restart (persistent decoration)

  5. **Hachi Obstacle Course**
     - Temporary ramps, rings, and platforms materialize in a street
     - Timed obstacle course — not competitive, just for fun
     - Completing it grants a "Parkour Pup" badge (first time) + points

  6. **Golden Hour (ゴールデンアワー)**
     - Lighting shifts to a gorgeous warm sunset for 3 minutes
     - All stamps discovered during golden hour count double
     - Hachi gets a temporary golden shimmer
     - Audio: lo-fi ambient track fades in

- **Event Announcements:** Subtle audio cues + small UI indicator (not intrusive). Reward players who are paying attention to the world, not staring at menus.

## How It Connects to Existing Systems

| Existing System | How Living Shibuya Enhances It |
|---|---|
| **Hachi evolution** | Evolution unlocks exploration spots (wall-run, double-jump areas) — not just race advantage |
| **Play Points** | Earned from NPC interactions, stamp discovery, event participation — not just matches |
| **Daily Missions** | New mission types: "Discover 3 stamps", "Attend a fireworks show", "Visit the cat colony" |
| **Cosmetics/Shop** | Stamp set rewards + event-exclusive temporary effects add to cosmetic variety |
| **Lobby downtime** | Lobby IS the game now — exploration and events happen between matches |
| **Match queue** | Players can explore freely while queued; events don't interrupt queue |

## Target Audience & Appeal

- **Primary (9-15 yo):** Discovery, cute Hachi reactions, photo mode, collecting stamps
- **Older casual players:** Ambient vibes, cozy atmosphere, lo-fi golden hour aesthetic
- **Japanese culture fans:** Stamp rally (instantly familiar), bon odori, matsuri atmosphere, authentic Shibuya details
- **Content creators:** Photo mode + fireworks + golden hour = screenshot/clip moments

---

## Unique Selling Points

- **"The Roblox game where the lobby is the best part"** — flips the party game formula
- **Stamp rally in a virtual Tokyo** — culturally authentic mechanic that hasn't been done well on Roblox
- **Hachi as emotional anchor** — ambient reactions make players feel like they have a companion, not just a vehicle

- **Animal Crossing** — ambient NPC life, time-based events, cozy collecting

**Size:** Medium-Large (phased delivery recommended)

**Phase 1 (4-6 weeks):** Day/night cycle, 4-5 NPCs with idle routines, Hachi ambient reactions (4 reaction types), 15 stamp spots, stamp UI, 2 micro-events (food truck + fireworks)

**Phase 2 (3-4 weeks):** Remaining NPCs, 15 more stamp spots, 4 more micro-events, photo mode, stamp set cosmetic rewards, new daily mission types

**Phase 3 (2-3 weeks):** Seasonal stamp rotation system, ambient audio overhaul, weather effects (rain), golden hour event, polish pass

## Key Development Challenges

1. **Performance budget** — NPC routines + ambient audio + lighting changes must not tank mobile framerate. Solution: aggressive LOD, NPC streaming (only active near player), baked lighting transitions.

2. **Day/night cycle vs match lighting** — Minigame arenas need consistent lighting for fairness. Solution: matches use fixed lighting; day/night only applies to lobby/exploration areas.

3. **Stamp spot placement** — 30+ spots need to feel genuinely hidden without being frustrating. Solution: difficulty tiers (easy/medium/hard), subtle environmental hints (a cat sitting near a hidden path).

4. **Event timing vs match queue** — Events shouldn't punish players who are about to enter a match. Solution: events are opt-in, short, and give rewards even for partial participation. Queue notification takes priority.

5. **NPC interaction on mobile** — Touch targets need to be generous. Solution: proximity-based auto-interaction (walk Hachi close → interaction triggers), no precise tapping required.
